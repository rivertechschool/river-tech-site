Performing Arts

The Performing Arts live at the very heart of River Tech's identity. Music, drama, and dance awaken creativity, discipline, and joy in students, shaping both their character and their confidence. Through the arts, they learn to tell stories that matter, to move hearts, and to bring beauty into a world that desperately needs it.

At River Tech, we see artistic expression not just as entertainment but as a calling — an opportunity to reflect the creativity of the Creator. Every rehearsal, every performance, every note sung or line spoken is a step toward mastering both craft and spirit.

"Sing to him a new song; play skillfully on the strings, with loud shouts." — Psalm 33:3 ESV

Just as athletics trains the body and technology sharpens the mind, the arts shape the soul. Students discover the power of teamwork, perseverance, and imagination, developing gifts that will serve them in every area of life. Our vision is to raise up a generation that can move audiences, heal hearts, and glorify God through their creative expression.

Join us as we nurture performers who shine with excellence, courage, and wonder — artists prepared not only for the stage, but for every stage of life.

Our Arts Team

Dan Hegelund — Arts Director

Dan Hegelund, principal and arts director at River Tech, is a professional vocal coach, choir director, music director, etc. He has thirty years of experience teaching and producing music and heads the River Tech Arts department. Expect concerts, musicals, studio recordings, music videos, choir trips, talent shows, music festivals, etc.

Caitlin Relvas — Dance Director

Caitlin Relvas has a B.A. in English and Classical Civilizations. She has a minor in Dance and has been studying and teaching dance for most of her life. Caitlin loves to teach K-1 students problem-solving skills and responsibility and teach middle and high school English students the art of writing and an appreciation for story.

MUSICALS

At River Tech School of Performing Arts & Technology, our annual musicals are a celebration of talent, teamwork, and imagination. Every week, students dive into singing, dancing, and acting, while a behind-the-scenes crew brings the magic to life with props, sets, and lighting. Open to anyone willing to audition and commit, each musical challenges students to push their skills further, resulting in productions that are bigger, bolder, and more spectacular each year. From the first rehearsal to the final curtain call, River Tech musicals are where creativity soars and every student has a chance to shine.

INSTRUMENTS

At River Tech School of Performing Arts & Technology, students can dive into the world of music by learning instruments like piano, guitar, and drums. Weekly lessons build strong foundational skills, while bands and our worship team give students the chance to perform, collaborate, and lead music in real settings. Whether jamming with friends, learning new pieces, or performing at assemblies, River Tech provides an inspiring environment for students to grow their musical talents and discover the joy of making music together.

OUR MUSICAL PERFORMANCES

December, 2025: Frozen

"Frozen" — A Magical Musical Adventure at River Tech

Frozen promises an enchanting experience, with breathtaking band performances and captivating dance routines that bring this cherished tale to life. Performances will take place on December 10th and 11th at 6:00 PM. If your child thrives in the spotlight, don't miss this chance to join a magical, unforgettable production.

Reminder: Join us for our Fun in the Sun Summer Concert—Indoors on May 22nd at 6:00 PM at River Tech, and Outdoors on May 28th at 6:00 PM at Coeur d'Alene City Beach.

NEW: Homeschoolers enrolled in our one- or two-day homeschool program are eligible to participate in this musical.

December, 2024: Journey to Bethlehem

"Journey to Bethlehem" was a remarkable production, taking our band performances and dance routines to new heights. We told the timeless story of Jesus' birth to two packed audiences. What a memorable way to celebrate Christmas.

April, 2024: The Greatest Showman

"The Greatest Showman" — A Harmonious Blend of Talent at River Tech

"The Greatest Showman," was a vibrant showcase of how far we've come since our first musical.

In "The Greatest Showman," we featured an expanded repertoire of songs and dances, reflecting the substantial growth in our students' artistic talents. The band and dance troupe collaboratively reached new heights, creating a synchronized spectacle of sound and movement.

Equally noteworthy was the remarkable development of our vocalists. Multiple soloists took the stage, each showcasing their significantly enhanced vocal performances.

June, 2023: The Lion King

"The Lion King" — Raising the Bar at River Tech

River Tech School's production of "The Lion King" set a new standard for our musical performances. This ambitious rendition featured 16 lapel microphones, ensuring crystal-clear audio, and showcased the talents of not one, but two bands, adding a rich, immersive depth to the musical score.

Our students took on several advanced dance routines, which brought the vibrant world of the savannah to life on stage. The extended script allowed our actors more space to develop their characters and engage the audience.

A special mention must be made of Daenerys, who, in her role as Timba, captured the hearts of the audience. Her impeccable comedic timing and expressive performance frequently had the audience erupting into laughter, making her portrayal a standout highlight of the show.

This production was not just a display of our growing expertise in theater but also a celebration of the hard work, creativity, and spirit of collaboration that defines River Tech.

December, 2022: Annie

"Annie" — A Leap Forward in Musical Theater at River Tech

With our production of "Annie," River Tech School took a bold step forward in the world of musical theater. This beloved classic brought new challenges and opportunities, as we started incorporating more complex elements like dance routines and live band performances.

Our students shone brightly, embracing the rhythmic dance numbers with enthusiasm and skill. The addition of a live band added a vibrant, dynamic layer to the show, filling the auditorium with energy and excitement.

A significant technical advancement was the introduction of lapel microphones, enhancing the clarity and reach of our actors' voices.

"Annie" was more than just a musical; it was a journey of growth and exploration in performing arts for River Tech. Each song, dance, and line delivered by our talented students was a testament to their dedication and passion, making "Annie" a memorable and inspiring experience for our school community.

June, 2022: The Jungle Book

"The Jungle Book" — Our First Step into the Spotlight

Our inaugural musical, "The Jungle Book," was a delightful 20-minute journey into the realm of theatrical arts at River Tech School. This performance was a vibrant display of costumes, makeup, and song, introducing our students to the magic of musical theater.

Evelyn's standout performance as King Louie not only captivated the audience but also set a high standard for our future productions. Her portrayal embodied the spirit and whimsy of her character, leaving a lasting impression.

This production marked the beginning of our journey in the performing arts, fostering a newfound appreciation and enthusiasm for musical theater within our school community. "The Jungle Book" was not just a performance; it was the start of an enduring legacy of creativity and collaboration at River Tech.
