Idaho Parental Choice Tax Credit

The application window is open now

River Tech is a participating school in the Idaho Parental Choice Tax Credit program, signed into law on February 27, 2025. This program offers up to $5,000 per child (or $7,500 for special needs students) to help cover tuition.

What Is the Idaho Parental Choice Tax Credit?

Idaho families can receive up to $5,000 per student (or $7,500 for students with disabilities) as a refundable tax credit for qualifying education expenses in 2025. This includes private school tuition, tutoring, curriculum, textbooks, assessments, and transportation.

Refundable means that even if you owe little or no state income tax, you can still receive the full benefit. You pay tuition throughout the year, then receive the credit when you file your 2025 tax return.

Does River Tech Qualify?

Yes, but it depends on which program your child is enrolled in:

ProgramQualifies?
4–5 day students (full-time program)✅ Yes — qualifies under K–12 tuition at a non-public school
1–2 day students (homeschool program)❌ Does not qualify

Important Details

  • Income Priority: Families earning up to 300% of the federal poverty line (~$96,000 for a family of four) receive priority, but there is no income limit. All families can apply.
  • Enrollment Restrictions: Students enrolled in public school programs (including Harmony, Braintree, or HomeEd 360) are considered public school students and would need to disenroll to qualify.
  • Limited Funding: The program is capped at $50 million statewide, supporting approximately 10,000 students on a first-come, first-served basis.
  • School Requirements: To qualify, schools don’t need accreditation, just a journal tracking progress in core subjects. We track progress in all core subjects.
