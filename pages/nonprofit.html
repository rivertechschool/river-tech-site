Faithful Five

501(c)(3) Nonprofit

We’re glad you’re here! Faithful Five started as a simple family dream — a mom, dad, and their three kids wanting to make a difference. Today, we’re a 501(c)(3) nonprofit with a dedicated board of caring individuals. We run River Tech School, where students thrive through personalized learning, and extend compassion to our community through the Jesus People Coffee House, a welcoming space offering support and resources to those experiencing homelessness.

Our mission is to empower lives through faith-based education and heartfelt outreach. Join us in building a stronger, more compassionate community together.

Our Journey and Mission

It all began around our family kitchen table. We — Dan, Mary, and our three children — felt a deep calling to serve others in meaningful ways. What was once a family dream has grown into Faithful Five, a nonprofit run by a handful of dedicated board members who share our passion for making a positive impact.

River Tech School is at the heart of what we do. As a 1–12 Christian school, we focus on personalized learning and small class sizes to nurture each student’s unique gifts. By integrating technology and the performing arts, we’re preparing the next generation of creative thinkers and leaders. But our commitment doesn’t stop at education.

Through the Jesus People Coffee House, led by Linda Meissner from the pages of David Wilkerson’s book The Cross and the Switchblade, we reach out to those experiencing homelessness. Offering free coffee, food, clothing, and Christian support, we aim to meet immediate needs while also helping individuals find pathways to rehabilitation and renewed hope.

We’re more than an organization; we’re a community built on family values of kindness, faith, and service. Our board and team are committed to operating with transparency and integrity, ensuring that every effort contributes meaningfully to our mission.
